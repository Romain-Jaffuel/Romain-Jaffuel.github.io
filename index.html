Project 2: Drone Classification in Ukraine using Machine Learning (April 2024)

In the context of modern warfare, the use of military drones in Ukraine has become a major strategic issue. This project focuses on applying machine learning to classify different types of drones, whether for reconnaissance or as attack weapons. Real-time classification enhances the ability to respond to enemy strategies. The project began with an OSINT phase to gather coordinates of Russian bases and technical specifications of drones. A Random Forest model was trained to classify 1000 simulated drone trajectories. This work contributes to the broader transformation of warfare through AI and automation. Project 3: Online Learning Statistics (June 2024)

This study analyzed learner engagement in MOOCs, focusing on the influence of sociodemographic factors like gender and Human Development Index (HDI). The goal was to identify major determinants of engagement, measured by the number of videos watched and quizzes completed. Data came from three MOOC editions, including activity logs and survey responses. The quantitative approach used t-tests and logistic regressions to evaluate main effects and interactions. Results showed a strong positive correlation between HDI and video engagement. Gender had no significant effect. These findings suggest that socio-economic context plays a larger role in learner behavior than gender, highlighting the importance of targeted interventions for underserved learners.
